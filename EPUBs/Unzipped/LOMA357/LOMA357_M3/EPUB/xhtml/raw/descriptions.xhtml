[A line graph appears with “Expected Return” on the Y axis and “Risk (Standard Deviation) on the X axis. A purple arrow curves up along the Y axis as it moves right along the X axis.]

Figure 8.1

Diversification and Portfolio Risk

The chart illustrates the portfolio’s risk over a period of time. The total risk is comprised of specific risk and systematic risk. The level of total risk curves downward over time.

The level of systematic risk stays constant. Therefore, the decrease in specific risk over time is what causes the portfolio’s total risk to curve downward over time. Eventually, almost all of the specific risk is gone, leaving systematic risk to make up the majority of the total risk.

Video 8.1

Reactions to Upward Price Trend

[A line graph labeled “Time” on the X axis and “Price” on the Y axis. The line, labeled “Share Price,” moves upward on the graph and is lifted up by a group of people labeled “Market Sentiment.”]

Positive market sentiment causes this stock’s share price to rise.

Momentum investors notice the stock’s upward price trend and believe the trend will continue. They buy shares.

Contrarian investors see the price rise above the stock’s intrinsic value. They believe the upward trend will reverse, so they sell their shares.

Thus, the momentum investors are following market sentiment while the contrarian investors are taking the opposite approach.

This bond ladder consists of five bonds with varying maturity dates. Each maturity date is one year apart.

[A ladder appears labeled “Year One.” The first step matures one year from now. The second step matures two years from now. The third step matures three years from now. The fourth step matures four years from now. The fifth step matures five years from now.]

The first bond in the ladder matures after one year.

One year later, another bond matures and the proceeds are reinvested.

[A sixth step is added to the “Year Three” ladder. It matures five years from now.]

Whenever one bond matures, the investor purchases another bond with a five-year term to maturity, thus maintaining the bond ladder.

VaR

There is a vertical line at the 5% confidence level part of the curve. The value attached to this vertical line is negative $10 million.

The blue area to the right of the vertical line, which is the majority of the area under the curve, illustrates the 95% confidence level. This area signifies that the portfolio management team is 95% confident that the portfolio’s one-month loss will not exceed $10 million.

The red area to the left of the vertical line signifies the 5% chance that the loss exceeds $10 million.
