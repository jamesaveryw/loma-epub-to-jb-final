As you will see in the next three chapters, the process of managing portfolios for institutional investors is a continuous process. This chapter introduces the first part of this process: portfolio construction. Portfolio construction is the systematic approach to building a portfolio of asset classes and allocations in accordance with the portfolio’s objectives and constraints. Subsequent chapters will cover strategies for managing institutional portfolios and evaluating portfolio performance. Figure 7.1 outlines the portfolio management process.

In the first part of this chapter, you will read about how investment professionals generally construct portfolios for institutional investors. These concepts apply to the general account portfolios of life insurance companies.

Next, you will see a couple of examples of a hypothetical insurer investment function evaluating and selecting individual assets for its general account portfolio. Finally, this chapter introduces principles that insurers use to evaluate and select the fund options they provide with their separate account products.
