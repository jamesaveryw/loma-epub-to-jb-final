To ensure that insurers have sufficient assets to meet their contractual obligations when they come due, state insurance regulators generally require insurers to hold minimum amounts of capital and to follow specific asset valuation methods and financial reporting standards. With regard to state insurance requirements, it is correct to say

    1. that insurance regulators determine the minimum capital level requirements for an insurer based on the insurer’s policies in force and return on invested assets
    2. that subaccount investments held in an insurer’s separate accounts are exempt from SEC asset valuation regulations
    3. that insurers with investments in debt securities rated NAIC 6 have lower risk charges than insurers with investments in debt securities rated NAIC 1

    Under SAP, insurers in the U.S. are not allowed to report the full value of specified general account investments on the Assets page of the Annual Statement.   7. The following statement(s) can correctly be made about state regulations that affect insurance company investments in the United States:

    1. The Investments of Insurers Model Act (Defined Standards Version) specifies the types of assets that insurers are permitted to treat as admitted assets and imposes quantitative limitations on the amount of each type of asset an insurer may treat as admitted assets.
    2. The Asset Valuation Reserve (AVR) absorbs realized and unrealized investment gains and losses on assets sold, when such gains and losses are related to changes in credit quality or equity price fluctuations.
    3. The Asset Valuation Reserve (AVR) and the Interest Maintenance Reserve (IMR) are both asset accounts that affect only insurance company separate account investments.
    1. A, B, and C
    2. A and B only
    3. B and C only
    4. A only
    5. B only

    Statement A is incorrect. The Defined Limits Version gives companies specific and narrowly defined limits on specified categories in balancing portfolio risk exposures. Each limit represents an acceptable category of securities or issuers. The limits in the Defined Standards Version are broader than those of the Defined Limits Version, in that insurers have some discretion in selecting investments of varying risk exposures. Unlike the Defined Limits Version, the Defined Standards Version does not specify categories of securities and issuers that are acceptable to regulators.

    Statement B is correct. The AVR minimizes the effects of both realized and unrealized gains and losses on an insurer’s invested assets when those gains and losses are related to the credit quality of the assets or fluctuations in the prices of equity securities. The IMR helps absorb realized gains and losses on an insurer’s interest-bearing assets when those gains or losses are caused by changes in market interest rates, rather than changes in the credit quality of the assets.

    Statement C is incorrect. The AVR and IMR are liabilities, but they are not contractual reserves. They are noncontractual reserves that apply only under statutory financial reporting standards and concern only general account assets.

  3. A soft-dollar arrangement is a situation in which an investment professional directs client trades to a broker-dealer in exchange for receiving soft dollars, which the professional uses to obtain products or services—often research services—from the broker-dealer.

    1. True
    2. False

    Soft-dollar arrangements cause ethical issues when a client, whose trades are generating commissions for a particular broker-dealer, does not benefit from the services that the investment professional receives by using soft dollars. Because soft dollars are incorporated into trade expenses, clients may not be aware of their true transaction costs.

    Although soft-dollar arrangements are legal in many jurisdictions, the CFA Institute and securities regulators have guidelines regarding the use and disclosure of these arrangements. Some jurisdictions even consider a soft-dollar arrangement a breach of fiduciary duty when the clients involved pay more than the lowest available commission rate.

    The SEC generally considers brokerage services as ineligible for soft-dollar arrangements unless the situation meets certain requirements. An investment professional should be able to demonstrate to the SEC that soft dollars paid for services that ultimately benefitted the clients whose transactions generated those dollars. Otherwise, the SEC could take regulatory action against the investment professional.

  4. Consider the following insider trading activities engaged in by two investment professionals:

    • Activity 1—A broker purchased a large block of a particular security based on advance knowledge of several large pending orders of the same security from clients.
    • Activity 2—A trader allowed several investors to sell mutual funds at their closing prices after U.S. stock markets had closed.

    Select the answer choice that correctly identifies whether the insider trader violation described in Activity 1 and Activity 2.

      Activity 1 Activity 2
    A. front running trading ahead of research
    B. front running late trading
    C. collusion trading ahead of research
    D. collusion late trading

    Some notable unethical trading practices are insider trading, trading ahead of research, front running, late trading, and collusion. Front running, a type of insider trading, involves the unethical trade of a security by an investment professional based on advance knowledge of large pending client orders of the security.

    Late trading is a type of illegal preferential treatment given to some investors who are allowed to trade securities—typically open-end mutual funds—after a stock market closes using that day’s closing prices. Late trading enables certain investors to use the day’s closing prices to conduct transactions, which, in turn, are based on information that became available only after the market’s close. Late trading is illegal in the United States because investors use information that was not available when markets were open to gain an unfair advantage over other participants in the market.
