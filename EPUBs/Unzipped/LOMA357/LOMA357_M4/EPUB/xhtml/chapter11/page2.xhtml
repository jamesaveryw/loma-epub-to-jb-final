In the last chapter, you read about regulations designed to ensure that financial services professionals conduct business with customers in a fair manner. However, such regulations cannot anticipate every situation with the potential for professional misconduct or describe how a professional should behave in every situation. Therefore, professionals also rely on ethics: a system of accepted standards of conduct and moral judgment that combines the elements of honesty, integrity, and fair treatment. As you will see, ethical conduct benefits companies as well as their customers.

Ethical standards provide guidance to an insurance company’s employees and other professionals who act on its behalf. Certain ethical standards apply to an insurer’s investment function. In this chapter, you will learn about ethical and unethical practices for investment professionals. In addition, you will read about particular corporate policies and internal controls that supplement ethical standards in promoting proper conduct among investment function professionals.
