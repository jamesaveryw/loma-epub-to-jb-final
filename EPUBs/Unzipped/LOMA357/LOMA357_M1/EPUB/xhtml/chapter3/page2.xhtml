In the previous chapter, you read about some basic types of investment vehicles that life insurers and other institutional investors use. In this chapter, you will learn about additional types of investment vehicles that life insurers use in their portfolios—such as structured securities, derivatives, alternative investments, and investment funds.

Money flows in and out of investment vehicles through transactions in capital markets. A capital market is the aggregate of all marketplaces, participants, and activities that move funding in a given economic system from net suppliers of capital to net users of capital.

In the last part of this chapter, you will learn about capital markets, participants in capital markets, and types of capital market transactions.
