Example of an Integrated Portfolio Benchmarking Model

Figure 6.3

How to Create a Replicating Portfolio for Liabilities

There are three main steps for creating a replicating portfolio. Step one is to identify assets that exhibit the risk and return characteristics of the pool of liabilities.

Step two is to select market indexes that display the relevant characteristics of the selected asset classes.

Step three is to assemble the market indexes in suitable proportions to form a virtual portfolio.

Under the investment segmentation method, different product lines receive investment income from segmented assets. What does this income allocation process look like?

First, each product line receives premium income from its customers.

Next, each product line uses its premium income to purchase assets for the segment.

[Arrows move from each oval in the Product Lines column to the blocks in the Segmented Assets column.]

Each segment’s assets generate investment income for its respective product line.

[Arrows move from each block in the Segmented Assets column to the ovals in the Product Lines column.]

Each product line uses the investment income generated by its assets to pay its contractual obligations to its customers.

[Arrows move from each oval in the Product Lines column to the triangles in the Product Line Customers column.]
