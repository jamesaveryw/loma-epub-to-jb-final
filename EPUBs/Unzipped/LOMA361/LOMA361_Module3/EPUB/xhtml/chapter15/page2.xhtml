As individuals, we think about when we should save and when we should spend. We weigh essential purchases versus nonessential purchases. We consider saving and investments to ensure we have a sound financial future. A company makes similar decisions, both in the short term and in the long term. But insurers are responsible for the financial security of their business and they provide products and services that protect and/or preserve the financial security of their customers. When a company carries so much responsibility, it cannot make financial decisions randomly based on hunches and guesswork. All companies, especially those in the financial services industry, need a plan. Budgeting is an accounting process that includes creating a financial plan of action designed to help an organization achieve its goals. As you recall from Chapter 12, a budget is a financial plan of action, expressed in monetary terms, that covers a specified period, such as one year. A budget contains estimates of management’s expectations for the company’s performance over the next period. During the next period, the company can compare its actual results to expectations, and adjust its operations accordingly.
