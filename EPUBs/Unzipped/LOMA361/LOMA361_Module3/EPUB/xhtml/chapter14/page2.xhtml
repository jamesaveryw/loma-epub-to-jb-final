Management accounting is more than just record-keeping. Careful attention to an insurer’s costs offers insight into the company’s past performance, but it can also provide insights into a company’s possible future. By analyzing the way costs and revenues change over time, a company can extrapolate potential future risks or opportunities. If accounting tells a company what its financial situation is, financial analysis can examine why the company’s finances are performing in a certain way, or how the company might improve its financial situation. In this chapter, we examine several forms of financial analysis that insurers use for internal management purposes.
