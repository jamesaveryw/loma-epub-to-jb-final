So far in this text, our primary focus has been on financial accounting. Now, we shift our attention to management accounting. Recall that management accounting is the field of accounting that focuses primarily on reporting a company’s financial information to meet the needs of a company’s internal users, particularly its managers.

Although the information gathered by management accounting is key to certain external users, such as rating agencies, managers primarily use it to make informed decisions in each functional area by identifying, measuring, analyzing, and communicating financial information. Figure 12.1 provides some examples of these functions.

In the following three chapters, we explore other facets of management accounting of interest to life insurance companies: cost accounting, financial analysis, and budgeting. In this chapter, we lay the foundation by exploring responsibility accounting and the role it plays in decision-making.
