Every company, both inside and outside of the financial industry, has a keen interest in controlling costs. Cost accounting is a system for accumulating and categorizing expense data that is used to facilitate effective cost control and to generate accurate estimates of costs to use in the pricing of a company’s products. An insurer uses a cost accounting system to define, describe, accumulate, record, and assign all the costs the company incurs. If a company’s cost accounting is accurate and thorough, responsibility managers can make informed decisions with greater confidence. In this chapter, we discuss cost management and cost classification. We also describe the methods that companies use to allocate, analyze, and control costs.
