Performing the Accounting Function

The accounting profession generally recognizes two main areas of accounting: private accounting and public accounting. The two areas are generally distinguished from one another by whether their services are made available to the general public.

 

Private Accounting

Accountants who work in private accounting are company employees who prepare financial accounting reports and statements for the company’s external users, as well as management accounting reports for the company’s internal users.

Private insurance company accountants typically

  • Establish and maintain the insurer’s accounting records, as well as provide guidance on the insurer’s accounting policies

  • Keep records associated with all business transactions involving the insurer’s receipt or disbursement of money

  • Develop and maintain adequate internal controls to safeguard the insurer’s assets and to ensure the accuracy of financial statements

  • Prepare and analyze financial statements and financial reports

  • Interpret and analyze operating costs and budgeted amounts

The head of a company’s accounting function is typically the controller or comptroller. Although the controller usually reports to the company’s chief financial officer (CFO), reporting relationships among the controller and other company officers and employees vary among companies.

Public Accounting

Public accounting is performed by public accountants, who work for, or are affiliated with, a public accounting company or governmental regulatory authority. In the United States, accountants who work in public accounting generally earn the professional accounting credential known as the Certified Public Accountant (CPA) designation.

Although public accountants can perform activities related to both financial and management accounting, in this text, we emphasize public accountants’ financial accounting activities. For example, insurance companies hire public accountants to perform annual audits of their financial statements. An audit is the examination and evaluation of a company’s accounting records, policies, and procedures to ensure that certain requirements are met. We discuss auditing in detail later in the text.
