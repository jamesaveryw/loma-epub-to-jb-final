Accounting requires the utmost accuracy and clarity. A simple accounting error can have a tremendous negative impact on a company, with consequences for employees and customers alike. The accounting function must ensure that each report and financial statement provides useful information that serves as a solid basis for decision-making. To meet this goal, a company adheres to certain concepts and principles concerning the

  • Type of accounting information that is recorded and reported

  • Timing of recording and reporting accounting information

  • Processes related to the recording and reporting of accounting information

These concepts and principles contribute to predictive quality, which is a characteristic of accounting information that enables interested users to form a reasonably accurate estimate of a company’s financial strength or earnings potential. They are also vital to the statutory accounting objective of meeting the needs of regulators.
