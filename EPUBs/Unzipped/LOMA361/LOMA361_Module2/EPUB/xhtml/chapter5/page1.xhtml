Objectives

  • 5A Describe asset valuation methods based on cost and those based on fair value
  • 5B Distinguish between realized capital gains (losses) and unrealized capital gains (losses)
  • 5C Explain the typical process of accounting for an asset that has become permanently impaired
  • 5D Differentiate between an insurer’s admitted assets and nonadmitted assets and explain the importance of these classifications in statutory reporting
  • 5E Identify examples of a life insurance company’s invested assets, including bonds, mortgages, and stocks
  • 5F Describe typical accounting entries used to record transactions involving an insurer’s invested assets, such as bonds, mortgages, and stocks
  • 5G Describe the regulations that apply to derivatives held in insurance company portfolios and the typical accounting treatment for derivatives
