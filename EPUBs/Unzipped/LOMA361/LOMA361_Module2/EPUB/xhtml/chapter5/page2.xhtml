The previous four chapters covered the foundation of accounting concepts and practices. Beginning with this chapter, we discuss financial accounting, which focuses primarily on the external uses of an insurer’s financial information. We use the five major account classifications—assets, liabilities, owners’ equity, revenues, and expenses—to show key concepts and practices related to financial accounting and reporting in life insurance companies. Chapter 5 presents important information about assets, with an emphasis on the typical invested assets of life insurers—bonds, mortgages, and the stock of other companies. We describe common transactions for each type of invested asset, along with the appropriate accounting entries. We also briefly discuss derivatives.
