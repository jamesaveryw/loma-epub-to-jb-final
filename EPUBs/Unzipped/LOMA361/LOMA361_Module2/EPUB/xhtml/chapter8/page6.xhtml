Death Benefit Payments

A death claim is a request for payment upon the death of the insured, under the terms and conditions of a life insurance policy. Supplementary contracts can also provide for payments. Typically, a supplementary contract, also known as a settlement contract, arises from the election of a settlement option by a life insurance beneficiary. Payments on supplementary contracts are typically called contractual payments. In this section, we discuss accounting and financial reporting for death benefit payments and contractual payments associated with life insurance policies. Typical accounts used to record and report death benefit payments include Death Benefit Payments, Death Claims Paid, or Claim Settlements.

To comply with statutory reporting requirements, insurers in the United States must list the total liability for unpaid life insurance claims—that is, any claims that are unpaid at the end of the accounting period—on the Liabilities, Surplus and Other Funds page of the Annual Statement. Examples of unpaid life insurance claims include claims that are in the course of settlement and claims that are in dispute. The amount of an insurer’s liabilities for claims reported in the financial statements usually equals the aggregate amount of all claims.

Most life insurance policies provide for payment of the policy’s face amount upon the insured’s death. However, in certain cases, the actual amount of the death benefit payable differs from the policy’s face amount. For example, universal life insurance policies may provide a benefit larger than the policy’s face amount. In these cases, the insurer makes adjustments to the amount payable to a policy beneficiary.

Supplementary Contract Payments

As discussed earlier, a supplementary contract typically arises from the election of a settlement option by a life insurance beneficiary. In addition to the typical lump-sum settlement of policy proceeds, insurers provide settlement options, which are alternative methods that the beneficiary can elect for receiving payment of the policy proceeds. Typical settlement options for individual life insurance policies are the interest option, fixed period option, fixed amount option, and life income option.
