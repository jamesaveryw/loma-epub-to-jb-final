Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 7A Explain the importance of reserves and describe the role that actuaries play in establishing reserves
  • 7B Identify the types of accounting records commonly used in life insurance companies and the category of reserves associated with each type
  • 7C Differentiate between contractual reserves and noncontractual reserves
  • 7D Describe changes in how insurers estimate their GAAP reserves and statutory reserves
  • 7E Explain how insurers account for reserves in their accounting records and financial statements
  • 7F Explain the effect of reserve strengthening and reserve destrengthening on an insurer’s surplus
  • 7G Describe how surplus strain can be relieved by the use of deferred acquisition costs (DAC), modified reserves, and reinsurance
