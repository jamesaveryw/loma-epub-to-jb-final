Project Portfolio Management

The effort to redesign EndiFirst’s self-service portal, our example from Chapter 4, was a project initiated to improve a process. Unlike a process, which is an ongoing series of operations or work activities, a project is a temporary, planned undertaking to achieve a specified result. Project management is a framework for directing projects to completion that involves (1) setting project goals, and (2) planning, monitoring, and controlling project tasks, resources, and costs to meet those goals. Without project management, most projects would fail.

EXAMPLE

Project Management Need

Suppose you and your colleagues receive an assignment to work on a project developing a new product line. The unit manager briefs you and your colleagues on what the product should deliver and the expected completion date (“the end of the year”). Then, the unit manager leaves your group to perform the activities involved. She will check in on your group’s progress every few months.

The only clear goal is to deliver the product, but the steps involved are uncertain. With no formal schedule or budget, you and your colleagues don’t know which tasks to complete by when. You also don’t know which resources you need until you begin a task, meaning every task is put on hold until you acquire the necessary resource. With no formal meetings or status updates, changes go unaddressed until they cause issues.

When the unit manager reviews your group’s progress, she identifies incomplete steps and suggests that the team start over. Unfortunately, the group didn’t properly document each step. As a result, the group can expect to run into the same issues the second time around.

Effective project management would have minimized the issues in this example. However, ineffective project management is nearly as risky as no project management at all. A study by PwC reviewed over 10,000 projects from 200 companies in 30 countries and across multiple industries. They found that only 2.5 percent of companies successfully completed 100 percent of their projects. 1 Project failures cost companies money and offer customers no value, so companies need to prioritize project success. In the context of operational risk, project management is important because it allows companies to deliver optimal results, at the appropriate times, with the most efficient use of resources.
