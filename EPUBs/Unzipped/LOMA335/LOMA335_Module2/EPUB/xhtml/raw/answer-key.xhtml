  11. Use the following information to answer questions 10 and 11.

    Todd Lester developed the following network model to analyze a project:

    With regard to the amount of float in this project, it is correct to say that

    1. there is no float in this network model
    2. there is no float in path A-B-C-D-F-G-H
    3. the float for path A-B-C-D-F-G-H is 35 days
    4. the float for path A-B-E-G-H is 35 days

    Float, also known as slack time, is the length of time that an activity can be delayed without delaying the entire project. No activity on the critical path can have float, but any other network activity can have float. In this situation, A-B-C-D-F-G-H is not the critical path and does have float. The time for path A-B-C-D-F-G-H is 50 days, and the time for the critical path is 85 days; therefore, the float is 85 – 50 = 35.

  6. Pareto analysis and a fishbone diagram are two quality tools that companies can use to identify and analyze quality defects. According to the Pareto principle, (not all / all) factors in a situation contribute equally to an outcome. In a fishbone diagram that arranges the problem chronologically, the most recent potential cause is closest to the (fish head / fish tail).

    1. not all / fish head
    2. not all / fish tail
    3. all / fish head
    4. all / fish tail

    The Pareto principle states that a small percentage of causes contributes to a large percentage of the results. A similar guideline, the 80-20 principle, expands the Pareto principle to other situations and states that, in any given situation, 20 percent of the components of a situation account for 80 percent of the outcome. As a result, when faced with a problem, a person or company should focus on the causes that contribute most to the problem. Resolving the most significant contributing cause or causes will produce a reasonably effective, if not perfect, solution.

    The fish head in a fishbone diagram is labeled with a name for the problem to be analyzed. The fish bones that extend diagonally behind the fish head represent potential causes of the problem, presented either in chronological order or in some other order logically suggested by the nature of the problem. A fishbone diagram can offer a focal point in quality circle meetings. The structure helps employees remain focused on the causes of problems rather than the symptoms.
