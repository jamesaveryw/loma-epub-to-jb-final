Process Basics

As defined in Chapter 1, a process is an ongoing series of operations, work activities, or tasks ordered in a definite sequence so that each step adds incremental value toward achieving a result. A business process is an ongoing method or system for responding to customer needs. A business process can span several functional areas of an organization or be limited to one unit. The result of a business process is typically to deliver something valued by a customer, such as a contractual commitment, the completion of a transaction, or better services. The insight below describes examples of business processes that occur in different life insurance functional areas. Although operational areas vary from one insurer to the next, the processes performed remain relatively consistent.

INSIGHT

Business Processes in Life Insurance Companies

Human resources

  • Hiring, onboarding, and evaluating employees
  • Coordinating administrative and training functions

Product

  • New product development
  • Product improvement

Marketing

  • Developing marketing materials
  • Introducing products to market

Sales

  • Acquiring new business
  • Assisting customers with applications

Office services

  • Providing support to the marketing team
  • Managing commission payments and incentives for the sales team

Actuarial

  • Managing the technical design of company products
  • Calculating policy reserve and policy dividend amounts

Underwriting

  • Evaluating applications
  • Classifying proposed insureds
  • Supporting intelligent underwriting

Customer service

  • Managing customer information
  • Answering customer questions
  • Resolving customer concerns

Information technology

  • Securing IT systems
  • Addressing computer issues
  • Developing software applications

Contract administration

  • Collecting premiums
  • Processing payments
  • Maintaining records

Claims administration

  • Evaluating, approving, and administering claims

Accounting

  • Managing budgets to maintain solvency for stakeholders
  • Financial reporting

Investments

  • Managing company investments according to guidelines established by management

Legal

  • Representing the company in legal matters
  • Reviewing life insurance and annuity policy forms
  • Drafting contracts

Compliance

  • Ensuring operations comply with laws and regulations to operate ethically and securely
