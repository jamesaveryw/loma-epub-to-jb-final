Beyond Quality

At the beginning of this chapter, we noted that improving the customer journey is the ultimate goal for quality management in insurance. Quality management helps companies reach this goal by reducing defects and better satisfying customer needs.

Companies can differentiate themselves from other high-quality competitors by taking advantage of what matters to their customer base, even before the customer is aware of it. In the chapters to come, we discuss using research, analytics, and innovative thinking to remain ahead. Two other methods for standing out among competitors include

  • Improving internal culture. In the past, customers saw the brand that a company wanted them to see. Today, a company’s internal culture influences its brand. 8 The social sphere has created employees who reflect their work experiences to the outside world. An employee could express his concerns publicly if he feels unfairly treated or compensated, or if he identifies unethical business practices. Even if he addresses his concerns privately (for example, through email), those concerns could end up in the public domain. If fear or resentment among employees goes unaddressed, a negative company culture could come across in employees’ online presences or former employees’ reviews. As a result, customers today have access to a company’s internal culture. Companies can use this to their advantage: it creates the opportunity to strengthen brand and operations simultaneously. A company with a reputation for a positive internal culture will garner greater respect and, ultimately, more customer loyalty.

  • Establishing a purpose-driven brand. Today’s customers want their money connected to positive impact. As a result, they are wary of brands whose only purpose is financial. In fact, research from 2017 shows that 88 percent of customers expect brands to have a purpose with a positive impact. 9 Customers also avoid brands whose values don’t align with their own. A 2015 study from Nielsen reported that 66 percent of global consumers would pay more for sustainable products. 10 A global perspective, a dedication to social issues, and a dedication to sustainability are some of the business values that most appeal to customers. Above all, customers need to believe the business purpose, which means a company’s values have to ripple throughout the organization and into the world.

Delivering quality is essential to remaining competitive. Going beyond quality is essential to remaining ahead. To remind customers why they made the right purchase decision, companies not only need to meet customers’ needs, but also anticipate customers’ wants.
