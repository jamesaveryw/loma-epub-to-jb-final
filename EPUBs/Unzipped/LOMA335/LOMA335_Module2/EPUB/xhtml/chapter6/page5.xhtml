Quality Management Approaches

Although quality management and total quality management (TQM) are based on comparable principles, they are not interchangeable terms. Quality management simply refers to the actions an organization undertakes to meet customer expectations for quality. Total quality management (TQM) is a specific, process-oriented approach to quality management for creating an organizational culture committed to continuous improvement. Generally, TQM requires strict adherence to four principles:

  • Doing things right the first time

  • Being customer-centric

  • Building teamwork

  • Making continuous improvement a way of life

The primary advantage of TQM is that it provides a gradual way for companies to improve the quality of their operations, products, and services. Its primary drawback is its rigidity. TQM relies on upper management for close control and supervision. In addition, TQM is designed to mitigate risk by making incremental changes and “doing things right the first time.” The emphasis on risk mitigation can discourage agile thinking and large-scale innovation. 7

Keep in mind that TQM is one of many possible approaches to quality management. The insight below is an abridged list of quality management approaches, including those we described in detail in Chapter 4. Companies should evaluate these approaches and choose whichever approach or combination of approaches suits their needs.

INSIGHT

Approaches to Quality Management

  • Total Quality Management: Process-oriented approach for creating an organizational culture committed to continuous improvement
  • Continuous Quality Improvement: Approach to quality management that maintains that a company, even the most successful, can improve in all areas at all times
  • Agile Management: Approach to business effectiveness focused on finding and seizing opportunities to improve operations and processes
  • Six Sigma: Disciplined approach for defining, measuring, analyzing, improving, and controlling quality by reducing process defects so that results fall within established parameters
  • Lean Management: Continuous process improvement approach that emphasizes creating a value stream comprising Lean processes, as well as identifying non-value-adding activities and eliminating them
  • Lean Six Sigma: Hybrid improvement approach focused on minimizing waste and eliminating defects
  • Kansei Engineering: Approach to improving quality by integrating the psychological needs of customers into initial product design
  • Business Process Reengineering: Formal program for undertaking efforts to fundamentally redesign an organization’s business processes to improve business performance
  • Top-Down Approaches: Approaches wherein management indicates which quality areas need improvement, and employees work toward the improvement with existing tools
  • Bottom-Up Approaches: Approaches wherein employees indicate which quality areas need improvement, and management facilitates the improvement process
  • Object-Oriented Quality and Risk Management: Statistical model for forecasting the risk and quality associated with a product
  • TRIZ: Problem-solving approach based on data and forecasting
  • Kaizen: Gradual approach to process improvement using a series of modest improvements and feedback from process team members
  • Statistical Process Control: Statistical method for measuring and controlling quality during product manufacture
  • Kanban: Lean approach to increasing speed of delivery by using workload control to maximize workflow
  • Taguchi Methods: Statistical methods for improving the functional design of products
