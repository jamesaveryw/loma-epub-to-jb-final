To implement an effective risk management strategy or to develop a profitable product, an insurer needs an accurate estimation of its future financial situation. While it’s impossible to know exactly what the future holds, the process of financial modeling allows an insurer to make estimates that can serve as the foundation for its business strategies. As you recall from Chapter 1, a financial model is a computer-based mathematical model that approximates the operation of real-world financial processes. Insurers use financial models to solve problems, test scenarios, assess potential outcomes, and make informed decisions.
