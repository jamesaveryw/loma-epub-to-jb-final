Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 4A Identify and describe the four primary components of financial models
  • 4B Describe the role of variables in financial modeling
  • 4C Distinguish between fixed, dynamic, and stochastic assumptions
  • 4D Explain how insurers use experience data and estimates of future conditions, including trend analysis and futures studies
  • 4E Describe the factors that affect the quality of model inputs
  • 4F Describe and distinguish between the modeling approaches of deterministic, stochastic, and optimization modeling
  • 4G Explain how insurers interpret model outputs such as point estimates, range estimates, and probability distributions
  • 4H Describe scenario testing and its applications, such as dynamic solvency testing, asset adequacy analysis, and cash flow testing
  • 4I Describe sensitivity analysis and compare and contrast it to scenario testing
