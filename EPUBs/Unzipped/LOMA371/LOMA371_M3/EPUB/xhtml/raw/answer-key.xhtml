  10. The Dignity Life Insurance Company currently earns 8% on the investments supporting its fixed deferred annuity products. Dignity credits these products with 5% interest.   13. The Tiller Life Insurance Company is evaluating the internal rate of return (IRR) of two life insurance products that Tiller is considering developing. Product X has a projected IRR of 17% and Product Y has a projected IRR of 12%. Each product has a risk-adjusted minimum IRR of 14%. Select the answer choice that correctly identifies whether Tiller most likely would proceed with developing Product X and Product Y.

      Product X Product Y
    A. yes yes
    B. yes no
    C. no yes
    D. no no

    Insurers that wish to account for the level of risk in a product’s cash flows set a risk-adjusted minimum IRR for a product and then compare the product’s projected IRR to the risk-adjusted minimu & bsp;IRR. A simple decision rule is to require that the projected IRR be greater than or equal to the insurer’s risk-adjusted minimum IRR for the product. If the projected IRR is less than the risk-adjusted minimum IRR, then the insurer will likely not proceed with developing the product as is.

    In Tiller’s situation, Product X has a 17% projected IRR, which is greater than the product’s risk-adjusted minimum IRR of 14%. Product Y has a 12% projected IRR, which is less than the product’s risk-adjusted minimum IRR of 14%. Thus, Tiller most likely will develop Product X but not Product Y.

    Some insurers refer to the IRR as the return on investment (ROI). For example, an insurer determines that the NPV for its proposed new product would be equal to zero when the interest rate is 12%; therefore, the projected IRR for the product is 12%.

    An insurer can instead use its hurdle rate as the required IRR. A hurdle rate, or required return, is a minimum percentage rate of return on capital that an insurer must earn to cover its cost of capital. Although the methods used to calculate the cost of capital vary, they generally involve a combination of the various interest costs, transaction costs, and cost of equity an insurer pays for funds. An insurer’s hurdle rate is independent of product risk, and typically applies to all products and all lines of business.
