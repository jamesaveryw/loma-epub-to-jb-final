Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 9A Identify the primary internal and external sources from which insurance companies gather expense data
  • 9B Distinguish between full costing and marginal costing and calculate expenses for a product using full costing and marginal costing
  • 9C Describe some of the methods that insurance companies use to determine the amount of direct and indirect expenses to assign to a particular product or product line
  • 9D Explain how insurers manage commission expenses for life insurance and annuity products
  • 9E Describe how policyholder behavior affects life insurance and annuity product design and modeling
  • 9F Decide whether a given product characteristic is associated with a smaller or a larger margin
  • 9G Explain how insurers incorporate operating expense charges into the product design of fixed and variable products
  • 9H Describe the strategies that life insurance companies use for managing the risks in life insurance and annuity products
