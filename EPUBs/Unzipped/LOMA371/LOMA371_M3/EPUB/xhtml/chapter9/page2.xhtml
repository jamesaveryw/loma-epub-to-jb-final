The cost of benefits is the main expense related to an insurance or annuity product. However, operating expenses and expenses associated with policyholder behavior also influence the profitability of such products. In 2018, operating expenses made up an average of 13% of insurers’ expenditures. 1 To ensure their products are profitable, insurers must incorporate operating expenses into product design and anticipate policyholder actions that would increase expenses or reduce income.

In Chapter 2, we stated that insurers build operating expenses into product design, often in the form of charges. The particular charges and processes vary from company to company, but the process generally includes four steps:

  • Describing the characteristics of and future policyholder behaviors associated with the operating expenses that are most likely to affect the product

  • Assigning relevant operating expenses to products and product lines

  • Controlling commission expenses and setting margins for risks and profits

  • Incorporating operating expenses into product design

In this chapter, we’ll describe these steps and look at product features that a technical product design team can build into complex products to mitigate risks, including policyholder behavior risks.
