Additional Risk Management for Complex Products

In previous chapters, we discussed the overall strategies that insurers use to manage risks. During product design, insurers can establish some design features that help to manage product risks, including certain forms of policyholder behavior risk.

For life insurance and annuity products, surrender charges are one example of a design feature implemented to combat risk. Other features include

  • Restricting policy loan activity.

  • Employing adjustable interest rates for policy loans.

  • Incorporating nonguaranteed elements in product designs. Recall that NGEs allow an insurer to reward customers when the insurer has experienced or anticipates it will experience favorable deviations from its assumptions, or, conversely, allow the insurer to charge customers more when the insurer has experienced or anticipates it will experience unfavorable deviations from its assumptions. NGEs can be nonguaranteed benefits or nonguaranteed charges. Common examples of NGEs include (1) policy dividends on participating fixed premium life insurance policies; (2) premium adjustments for indeterminate premium products; (3) excess interest on universal life insurance, fixed-rate deferred annuities, and other products that credit interest at current rates; and (4) adjustments in periodic charges deducted from accumulated values.

Insurers also use design features that help manage product risks for more specific types of products.

 

Fixed and Fixed Fund Products

For fixed-rate universal life insurance, fixed-rate deferred annuities, and variable products that offer fixed fund options, additional design adjustments that can help in controlling a company’s product risks include

  • Designing a lower minimum guaranteed interest-crediting rate, where regulations permit.

  • Shortening the guarantee period for minimum guaranteed interest-crediting rates.

  • Decreasing current interest-crediting rates within allowable limits.

  • Decreasing the interval between declarations of new interest-crediting rates on fixed-interest asset accumulations.

  • Limiting withdrawals of account values by monetary amount, frequency, and percentage of account value.

 

Variable Products

Design adjustments to mitigate risk for variable universal life insurance and variable deferred annuities include

  • Discouraging market-timing abuses and controlling asset-trading costs by limiting customers’ movements of contract values between subaccounts in a specified period (by means of prohibition or assessing additional charges).

  • Limiting new premium additions to fixed accounts in a specified period, such as per calendar quarter.

  • Limiting reallocations of existing fund values to fixed accounts, such as placing a maximum monetary amount limit per account or limiting total fixed account allocations to a percentage of total allocations.

  • Limiting withdrawals of account values by monetary amount, frequency, and percentage of account value.

 

Variable Deferred Annuities

To address specific risks associated with variable deferred annuities, insurers can adjust product designs in several ways such as

  • Requiring waiting periods before a contract owner can begin withdrawing guaranteed income from the contract.

  • Imposing restrictions on the kinds of investments that contract owners can select.

  • Applying age-based guaranteed withdrawals, in which contract owners receive a higher guaranteed withdrawal rate if they delay drawing income until a specified age.

  • Using index-based guarantees, which help reduce an insurer’s capital market risk.

  • Using asset transfer algorithms, which dictate the transfer of assets between equity and fixed-income investments, depending on the direction of the market.
