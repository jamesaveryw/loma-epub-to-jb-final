Keeping pace with change is important in every industry, but it’s especially crucial in the life insurance industry. Examples of changes that affect life insurers include

  • Technological and social changes that affect the types of products and services that customers want

  • Changes in mortality rates due to medical advances

  • Shifts in the economy that affect customers’ purchasing power

  • New competitors entering a market, offering products that raise customer expectations

  • Regulations that impose new requirements on life insurance products

To remain competitive in this environment, a life insurance company must be quick in determining customer needs and bringing products that meet those needs to market. An insurer must also be thorough in its product development process, ensuring that each new product is meticulously researched and supported by an efficient use of company resources.

To create new products quickly enough to meet changing customer needs, a company needs strong planning and efficient processes. In this chapter, we discuss the considerations insurers address when developing new products, as well as the importance of product management and control. We also describe the first two steps in the typical product development process: product planning and comprehensive business analysis. We will discuss the remaining three steps—technical product design, product introduction, and product monitoring—in the following chapter.
