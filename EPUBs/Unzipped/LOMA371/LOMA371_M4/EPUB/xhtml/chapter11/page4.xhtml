Annuity Benefits Overview

In general, annuities potentially provide the following benefit types: death benefits, surrender and withdrawal benefits, periodic income payments, and guarantee riders.

One way to look at the benefits under annuities is by contract period. The benefits offered by deferred annuities differ between the accumulation period and the payout period. For example, deferred annuities allow for surrender and withdrawal benefits during the accumulation period, but do not usually provide such benefits during the payout period. Recall that immediate annuities do not have an accumulation period. During the payout period, immediate annuities do not provide surrender and withdrawal benefits.

Another way to look at the benefits under annuities is by annuity type. For instance, fixed immediate annuities have a payout period, but no accumulation period. Therefore, such annuities do not pay death benefits or withdrawal benefits. Because fixed-rate and variable deferred annuities have an accumulation period, they have the potential to pay death benefits, surrender benefits, or withdrawal benefits.
