  5. Wanda Hubley purchased a fixed indexed annuity (FIA) contract with a single premium of $200,000. The FIA has a 75% participation percentage, a 10% cap, and no yield spread. Ms. Hubley chose the method of calculating index growth in which the value of the reference index at the end of each contract year is compared to the value of the index at the start of the year. For Ms. Hubley’s FIA, the reference index equaled 750 at the beginning of Year 1, 800 at the end of Year 1, and 875 at the end of Year 2. Select the answer choice that correctly identifies the method that Ms. Hubley chose for calculating index growth and the accumulated value of Ms. Hubley’s FIA at the end of Year 2.

      Index credit calculation method FIA value, end of Year 2
    A. annual reset method $210,000
    B. annual reset method $224,763
    C. high water mark method $210,000
    D. high water mark method $224,763

    Under the annual reset method, also known as the ratchet method, index values are reset at the beginning of each contract year during the term of the contract. The annual reset method allows the contract owner to lock in gains each year and avoid losses. When the market goes up, the contract owner earns excess interest. When the market remains stable or goes down, the contract neither gains nor loses value.

    To calculate the growth of Ms. Hubley’s FIA at the end of Year 1, calculate how much the reference index grew: (800 – 750) ÷ 750 = 6.67% at the end of Year 1. Adjusting the 6.67% growth in the index by the 75% participation percentage results in (6.67% × 0.75) = 5% interest. Because 5% is less than the 10% cap, the interest rate to apply to the FIA contract value at the end of Year 1 is 5%. The accumulated value of the FIA is $200,000 × 1.05 = $210,000, which is the FIA contract value at the beginning of Year 2.

    At the end of Year 2, the reference index grew by (875 – 800) ÷ 800 = 9.38%. Adjusting the growth in the index by the 75% participation percentage results in (9.38% × 0.75) = 7.03% interest. Because 7.03% is less than the 10% cap, the interest rate to apply to the FIA contract value at the end of Year 1 is 7.03%. The accumulated value of the FIA is $210,000 × 1.0703 = $224,763 at the end of Year 2, which is the FIA contract value at the beginning of Year 3.

    The point-to-point, annual reset, and high water mark methods allow the contract owner to take advantage of index gains without being subject to index losses. The advantage of using high water marks instead of methods that use term-end or year-end values is that earnings are not dependent on index growth at the end of each term (point-to-point method) or contract year (annual reset method). Under the high water mark method, the index need only show growth on any contract anniversary date to earn index credits.
