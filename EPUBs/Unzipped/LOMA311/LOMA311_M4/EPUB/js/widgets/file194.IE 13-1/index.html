Performing or Contesting a Life Insurance Policy

Let’s review some examples of claims practices. For each example, click on if you think the example is performing the policy; click on if you think the example is contesting the policy.

Sue Moon died in a crash while piloting an airplane during military service. Ms. Moon was insured by an individual life insurance policy that had an aviation exclusion. This policy provision excluded from coverage any death caused by aviation-related activities while in the military.

This is an example of performing the policy.

In performing the policy, the insurer denied payment of the claim because the cause of Ms. Moon’s death was not covered by her policy.

Stan Wood died two days after his individual life insurance policy began. While evaluating the death claim, the insurer discovered false information on Mr. Wood’s application for insurance that would have caused the insurer to deny the application had it known the truth.

This is an example of contesting the policy.

In contesting the policy, the insurer

  • denied payment of the claim
  • notified the claimant that the policy was invalid
  • returned all premiums paid

In response, the claimant filed an action in court to enforce the contract.

The insurer defended the lawsuit on the grounds that the policy was invalid as a result of material misrepresentation in the application.
