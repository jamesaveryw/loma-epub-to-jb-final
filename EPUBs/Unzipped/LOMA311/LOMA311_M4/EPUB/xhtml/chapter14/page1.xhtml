Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 14A Explain an insurer’s duties to perform annuity contracts according to the most common annuitized payout options as well as the terms of structured settlement agreements
  • 14B List the tasks that make up the standard claim procedures insurers follow when processing life insurance claims
  • 14C Describe how insurers determine the proper payee of life insurance policy proceeds in situations involving conflicting claimants, common disasters, and short-term survivorship
  • 14D Calculate the amount of policy proceeds payable under a life insurance policy
  • 14E Explain how insurers handle life insurance claim situations that may involve fraud as well as those that involve policy exclusions, disappearance of the insured, disqualification of the beneficiary, and accidental death benefits
  • 14F Describe how insurers can settle claim disputes through compromise settlements, and explain how conflict of laws rules apply in civil actions involving life insurance policies
