Recall that life insurance policies, annuities, and other financial products are types of intangible property. Each of these products is a bundle of property rights, most of which vest in the owner. A vested right is a right that cannot be altered or changed without the consent of the person who owns the right. People other than the owner may have, or may be personally granted, certain rights in such personal property. In this chapter, we discuss property rights as they relate to individual life insurance, annuity, and mutual fund products.
