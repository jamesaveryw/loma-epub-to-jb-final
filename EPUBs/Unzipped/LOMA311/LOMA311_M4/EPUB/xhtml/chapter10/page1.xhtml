Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 10A Analyze the process of offer and acceptance and identify other requirements the parties must meet to form a valid life insurance contract
  • 10B Explain the significance of the insurable interest requirement and the consent requirement, and describe how the parties meet these requirements
  • 10C Determine in a given situation whether an insurance policy would be considered to have been delivered to the policyowner
  • 10D Distinguish between a closed contract and an open contract, and identify the documents that constitute the entire contract between the insurer and the policyowner
  • 10E Analyze the various types of temporary insurance agreements, and explain how courts apply the doctrine of reasonable expectations to interpret premium receipts
  • 10F Identify the steps involved in the formation of annuity contracts
  • 10G Describe the types of activities that agency agreements typically authorize insurance agents to perform on behalf of an insurer
