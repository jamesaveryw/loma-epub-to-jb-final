Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 13A Distinguish between performance of a life insurance policy and contest of a life insurance policy
  • 13B Describe the grounds on which an insurer may contest the validity of a life insurance policy
  • 13C Describe the three elements of a material misrepresentation
  • 13D Explain the actions an insurer may take when it discovers a misrepresentation of the insured’s age or sex
  • 13E Explain how the incontestability provision limits an insurer’s right to contest the validity of a life insurance policy, and identify other barriers to contesting a policy
  • 13F Distinguish among the types of mistakes that can affect the validity of a life insurance policy
  • 13G Define common legal and equitable remedies available to the parties to a life insurance policy
