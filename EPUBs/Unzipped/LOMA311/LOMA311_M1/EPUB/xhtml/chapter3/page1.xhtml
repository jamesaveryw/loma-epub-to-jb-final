Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 3A Identify the characteristics of a corporation that make it the ideal form of business organization for an insurance company
  • 3B Explain the steps a life insurer must take to do business in the United States
  • 3C Compare characteristics of corporations in the United States and other countries
  • 3D Define a multinational corporation and describe ways in which companies can operate in a foreign country
  • 3E Compare the three primary groups of people who are involved in the management of a corporation and explain each group’s legal rights and duties
  • 3F Identify methods by which corporations can change their corporate structures
  • 3G Explain U.S. federal antitrust laws and the types of activities they typically prohibit, and compare U.S. federal antitrust laws to antitrust laws in other countries
  • 3H Describe federal consumer protection laws that regulate trade practices
  • 3I List the nondiscrimination requirements that U.S. federal and state laws typically impose on employers
