Institutional investors develop and execute plans for managing their investment portfolios. Before forming a plan, an institution considers the basic investment goals it is trying to achieve. Then the institution identifies specific return and risk objectives and investment constraints. All of this information is formalized in a document that guides the strategy of the investment professionals who implement the institution’s investment plan.

In this chapter, you will learn about the basic goals of investing, which apply to both retail and institutional investors. In addition, you will read about investment objectives, investment constraints, and investment policy statements for institutional investors. Later in the chapter, you will gain an understanding of some investment objectives and constraints specific to life insurance companies.
