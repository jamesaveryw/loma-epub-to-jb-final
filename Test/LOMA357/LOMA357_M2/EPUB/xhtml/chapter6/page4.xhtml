Organization of the ALM Function

The ALM function is responsible for conducting appropriate analysis, communicating the results of the analysis, recommending ALM policy, supporting ALM policy implementation, and establishing systems for ensuring that policies are implemented.

Insurance companies define the authority, roles, and accountabilities at various levels of the ALM process. While each company may have a different organizational structure for its ALM function, the function generally consists of a risk committee, an ALM committee, and an ALM unit.

 

Risk Committee

An insurance company’s board of directors establishes a permanent oversight committee to oversee the company’s ERM efforts. The risk committee, also known as the enterprise risk committee, assists the board of directors in supervising the management of the company’s assets, liabilities, capital, and risks.

The risk committee typically supervises the ALM function. The committee

  • Defines roles and assigns ALM responsibilities

  • Oversees the ALM structure and process

  • Makes decisions regarding ALM policy and implementation

The risk committee also supervises the ALM committee and coordinates that committee’s efforts within the broader ERM framework.

 

ALM Committee

The ALM committee is a cross-functional committee that reports to the board’s risk committee. Typically, the ALM committee includes senior-level personnel. Comprising selected actuarial and investment professionals, the ALM committee reviews ALM performance and recommends ALM policy changes to the risk committee.

 

ALM Unit

Most large companies also have an ALM unit that reports to the ALM committee. The ALM unit brings together actuarial and investment professionals who analyze ALM risks. Many of these professionals specialize in ALM-related job functions.

The ALM unit works at the operational level, implementing the ALM framework and performing ALM support activities. As such, the ALM unit is directly accountable for ALM performance.
