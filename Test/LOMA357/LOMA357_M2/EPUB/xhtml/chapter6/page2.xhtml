Generally speaking, insurance companies are in the business of managing risk. In addition to managing risks for their customers, they are skilled at managing their own risks. To manage risks, each company conducts risk oversight at the enterprise level and the functional levels.

The risks that insurance companies manage affect the strategies they use for their investment portfolios. In this chapter, you will learn about the (1) asset-liability management (ALM) process for life insurer general accounts, (2) tools that insurers use for ALM analysis, and (3) investment strategies related to ALM.
