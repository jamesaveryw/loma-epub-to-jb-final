Objectives

After studying this chapter, you should be able to

  • 9 A Explain why investment professionals monitor portfolios for style drift, allocations that require rebalancing, asset-liability management (ALM) considerations, and compliance to policies and regulations
  • 9 B Describe some of the investment performance reporting standards, such as Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS®) and asset valuation standards
  • 9 C Explain how investment professionals use total return and excess return as metrics to measure a portfolio’s investment returns
  • 9 D Explain how investment professionals use standard deviation, tracking error, beta, and Value at Risk (VaR) as metrics to measure the level of risk that a portfolio has accepted
  • 9 E Explain how investment professionals use the Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, information ratio, and Jensen’s alpha as metrics to measure a portfolio’s risk-adjusted returns
  • 9 F Identify and describe some of the internal and external reviews that investment professionals use when evaluating a portfolio’s investment performance
  • 9 G Describe how attribution analysis provides investment professionals with information regarding the factors that contributed to a portfolio’s returns
