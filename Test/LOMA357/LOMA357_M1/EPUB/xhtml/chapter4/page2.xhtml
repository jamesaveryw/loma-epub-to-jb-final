Investment vehicles offer investors several types of possible returns, which, in turn, are influenced by multiple factors. To determine the success of their investments, investors monitor their investment returns, which they can measure using different methods.

All investing activities involve some element of risk. Institutional investors need to balance their pursuit of returns with their responsibility to manage risks. Some risks apply to entire markets, while others apply to specific assets.

In this chapter, you will learn about return and risk concepts. First, you will read about types of investment returns and see how different factors influence returns. Next, you will learn about some of the ways that investors measure returns and investment risk. Finally, you will read about certain risks that are relevant to life insurance company investments.
