Misrepresentations Prohibited by the States

Let’s review some examples of statements that state insurance laws may prohibit because they are considered to be misrepresentations of a feature or aspect of an insurance policy. For each statement, click on if you think the statement is not prohibited; click on if you think the statement is prohibited.

The insurance agent says to a customer…

“This life insurance policy is a great way to save; it’s like a money market account.”

Prohibited. Life insurance and annuity contracts must not be misrepresented as “investments” or “savings” vehicles.

“This fixed annuity is similar to a certificate of deposit, but there are some differences you should be aware of. Let me show them to you.”

Not prohibited. Annuity contracts must not be compared to certificates of deposit or similar “savings” vehicles without pointing out the key differences.

“Many people think of their life insurance premiums as deposits.”

Prohibited. Premiums must not be misrepresented as “deposits” or “premium deposits.”

“The dividends this policy pays are a great way to save money.”

Prohibited. Policy dividends must not be misrepresented as “earnings” or “savings,” and no guarantees may be implied.

“Typically, the way this permanent life insurance policy works is that after the insured dies, the beneficiary receives the policy benefit and not the policy’s cash value.”

Not prohibited. Life insurance agents may not imply that the beneficiary of a permanent life insurance policy will receive both the policy death benefit and the policy cash value after the death of the insured, unless this is so.
